First, You Must Apply

Finding the application for a convention can be an adventure in itself. The first step is to visit their website and scroll the menu categories. You're looking for links that say "crew," "join our crew," "team application," or something along those lines. Also, you can follow a con on their social media and when they are looking for help, they will post the application link there.

Next, you fill out the application, which will ask you about your prior convention experience. If you have none, that's perfectly okay! We train you!

And then, inevitably, you wait. You check your email spam three times a day for that approval email! But, eventually, grasshoppa, it will come.

Read Before You Apply!

Alert Danger GIFfrom Alert GIFs

There is no guarantee of what position you will be assigned when you apply for a convention, especially if you're a newbie. Most of the time, if you've worked for a con before, they'll position you where they know you're experienced. However, even the O.G.s like me get relegated to line control, sometimes. Either way, you usually don't find out your position until you check in for your first shift. Visit the What & Why page to familiarize yourself with the roles you could be assigned.
